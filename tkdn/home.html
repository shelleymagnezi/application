So What is This About?

Stacey Reddick has been in the food service business in the Washington, DC metropolitan area for 30 years. She is celebrating 25 years as a culinary food vendor in the historic Montgomery Farm Women’s Cooperative market in Bethesda, Maryland. She has extensive long-term relationships with corporate and individual clients and food service entrepreneurs and suppliers. The owner’s roots stem from a Midwestern woman-owned family catering business founded in the 1930’s by her maternal and fraternal grandmothers. They were the preferred partners in helping many culinary entrepreneurs start, grown and move into their own food businesses.

Clientèle

The Kitchen Dreams Network shared use facility will help emerging and established entrepreneurs overcome their largest obstacle: the high cost of starting up or subleasing a licensed and fully equipped commercial kitchen.

We serve a diverse culinary clientèle of caterers, bakers, private chefs, food truck and farm market vendors, culinary school graduates, church groups, non-profits and social clubs. Clients only pay for what they use whether it is daily production runs or one time food service events.

Benefits

The Kitchen Dreams Network intentionally uses its resources to offer entrepreneurs opportunities for business growth that include workshops, seminars, certification, and assess to marketplaces and suppliers. The Kitchen Dreams Network also serves as a community hub for professional resources, healthy food innovation and initiatives in a common forum.

Our dream is to help you realize your dream at an exponential rate.

Participation

Each entrepreneur must:
  • Produce proof of current food handler’s certification
  • Meet a general liability insurance requirement
  • Have a valid Shared Kitchen User city license
  • Comply with a registration/application procedure which includes :
    • a security deposit,
    • termination of use policy,
    • and rules concerning daily use cleaning.
